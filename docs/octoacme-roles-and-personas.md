# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads ensure software quality through comprehensive testing strategies, test automation, and quality assurance processes. They work closely with developers, product managers, and project managers to maintain high standards throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design and implement test strategies and test plans
- Oversee test automation and manual testing efforts
- Define and track quality metrics and acceptance criteria
- Coordinate bug triage and regression testing
- Mentor team members on testing best practices
- Ensure test coverage meets quality standards
- Validate that features meet acceptance criteria before release

### Goals
- Minimize defects in production
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Reduce time to validate features
- Maintain high quality standards across all releases

### Typical Communication
- Participate in sprint planning and backlog refinement
- Daily standups to report testing progress and blockers
- Bug triage meetings with developers
- Test status updates in weekly delivery syncs
- Quality sign-off before releases

### Typical Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on testability, review test cases, coordinate bug fixes
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria, validate feature requirements
- **Project Managers**: Report testing status, flag quality risks, coordinate release readiness

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, automation, and deployment pipelines that enable reliable and efficient software delivery. They bridge development and operations through automation and observability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure as code and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Automate deployment and rollback procedures
- Ensure system reliability, security, and performance
- Support incident response and troubleshooting
- Optimize build and deployment processes

### Goals
- Minimize deployment time and manual intervention
- Maximize system uptime and reliability
- Improve observability and incident response
- Enable teams to deploy safely and frequently

### Typical Communication
- Infrastructure planning and capacity discussions
- Deployment coordination and release windows
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Security and compliance reviews
- Weekly updates on pipeline improvements

### Typical Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provide CI/CD support, troubleshoot build issues, optimize deployment workflows
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate deployment schedules, report infrastructure risks, plan capacity
- **Product Managers**: Discuss infrastructure requirements for new features, provide performance insights

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers research user needs, design intuitive interfaces, and validate solutions through user testing. They ensure products are usable, accessible, and deliver excellent user experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with developers on implementation
- Validate designs through user feedback
- Ensure accessibility standards are met

### Goals
- Maximize user satisfaction and usability
- Reduce user friction and error rates
- Create consistent, accessible experiences
- Validate designs with real user feedback

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with stakeholders
- User research findings presentations
- Collaboration sessions with developers on implementation
- Feedback sessions with product managers
- Usability testing reports and insights

### Typical Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature requirements, user problems, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Partner on implementation feasibility, design handoffs, and interaction details
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate design timelines, flag design dependencies

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and technical teams by analyzing requirements, documenting processes, and ensuring solutions meet business needs. They clarify scope, validate requirements, and facilitate communication between business and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create detailed functional specifications and user stories
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate that solutions meet business objectives
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT)
- Maintain requirements traceability

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete requirements
- Minimize scope ambiguity and rework
- Maximize business value delivered
- Facilitate effective stakeholder communication

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Documentation and specification reviews
- User story elaboration with developers
- Status updates to business stakeholders
- UAT coordination and validation

### Typical Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on product vision, translate high-level goals into detailed requirements
- **Developers**: Provide detailed specifications, clarify requirements, validate technical approaches
- **Project Managers**: Define scope, track requirements changes, support project planning
- **Stakeholder Representatives**: Facilitate requirements gathering, coordinate UAT

---

## Stakeholder Representative

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives act as the voice of business units, customers, or partner teams. They provide input on requirements, validate solutions, and ensure projects align with organizational needs and priorities.

### Responsibilities
- Represent stakeholder interests and priorities
- Provide business context and requirements input
- Review and approve project deliverables
- Participate in key project decision points
- Coordinate communication with broader stakeholder groups
- Validate solutions meet stakeholder needs
- Support change management and adoption

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value
- Maintain stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Facilitate smooth adoption of solutions
- Provide timely feedback and approvals

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and planning discussions
- Regular status updates and milestone reviews
- Requirements validation and acceptance review
- Change approval and priority discussions
- Go-live coordination and feedback

### Typical Interactions with Existing Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provide business priorities, validate product direction, approve roadmaps
- **Project Managers**: Participate in governance, approve changes, review project status
- **Business Analysts**: Collaborate on requirements, provide business context, participate in UAT
- **Developers**: Provide feedback during demos, validate technical approaches meet business needs
